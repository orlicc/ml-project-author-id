Once more the genial friend must be appealed to, and requested to examine the Reader on Tables II and III, and to make him not only _represent_ Propositions, but also _interpret_ Diagrams when marked with Counters. The Questions and Answers should be like this:-- Q. Represent "No x' are y'." A. Grey Counter in S.E. Cell. Q. Interpret Red Counter on E. partition. A. "Some y' exist." Q. Represent "All y' are x." A. Red in N.E. Cell; Grey in S.E. Q. Interpret Grey Counter in S.W. Cell. A. "No x'y exist" = "No x' are y" = "No y are x'". &c., &c. At first the Examinee will need to have the Board and Counters before him; but he will soon learn to dispense with these, and to answer with his eyes shut or gazing into vacancy.
