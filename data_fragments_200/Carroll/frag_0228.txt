[Work Examples Sec. =1=, 9-12 (p. 97); Sec. =2=, 1-20 (p. 98).] pg053 CHAPTER IV. _INTERPRETATION, IN TERMS OF x AND y, OF TRILITERAL DIAGRAM, WHEN MARKED WITH COUNTERS OR DIGITS._ The problem before us is, given a marked Triliteral Diagram, to ascertain _what_ Propositions of Relation, in terms of x and y, are represented on it. The best plan, for a _beginner_, is to draw a _Biliteral_ Diagram alongside of it, and to transfer, from the one to the other, all the information he can.
