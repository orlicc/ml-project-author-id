“Well, I’ve been pretty much so, too, Huck. They most always put in a dead man when they bury a treasure under a tree, to look out for it.” “Lordy!” “Yes, they do. I’ve always heard that.” “Tom, I don’t like to fool around much where there’s dead people. A body’s bound to get into trouble with ’em, sure.” “I don’t like to stir ’em up, either. S’pose this one here was to stick his skull out and say something!” “Don’t Tom! It’s awful.” “Well, it just is. Huck, I don’t feel comfortable a bit.” “Say, Tom, let’s give this place up, and try somewheres else.” “All right, I reckon we better.” “What’ll it be?” Tom considered awhile; and then said: “The ha’nted house. That’s it!” “Blame it, I don’t like ha’nted houses, Tom. Why, they’re a dern sight worse’n dead people.
