You must go and learn it again. Don’t you be discouraged, Tom, you’ll manage it—and if you do, I’ll give you something ever so nice. There, now, that’s a good boy.” “All right! What is it, Mary, tell me what it is.” “Never you mind, Tom. You know if I say it’s nice, it is nice.” “You bet you that’s so, Mary. All right, I’ll tackle it again.” And he did “tackle it again”—and under the double pressure of curiosity and prospective gain he did it with such spirit that he accomplished a shining success. Mary gave him a brand-new “Barlow” knife worth twelve and a half cents; and the convulsion of delight that swept his system shook him to his foundations.
