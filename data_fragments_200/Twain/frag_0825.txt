He found that in the best tavern, No. 2 had long been occupied by a young lawyer, and was still so occupied. In the less ostentatious house, No. 2 was a mystery. The tavern-keeper’s young son said it was kept locked all the time, and he never saw anybody go into it or come out of it except at night; he did not know any particular reason for this state of things; had had some little curiosity, but it was rather feeble; had made the most of the mystery by entertaining himself with the idea that that room was “ha’nted”; had noticed that there was a light in there the night before. “That’s what I’ve found out, Huck. I reckon that’s the very No. 2 we’re after.” “I reckon it is, Tom. Now what you going to do?” “Lemme think.” Tom thought a long time. Then he said: “I’ll tell you.
