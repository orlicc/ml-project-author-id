But—” He stopped and looked around slow, like he wished he could run across a friendly eye somewheres, and fetched up on the old gentleman’s, and says, “Didn’t _you_ think she’d like me to kiss her, sir?” “Why, no; I—I—well, no, I b’lieve I didn’t.” Then he looks on around the same way to me, and says: “Tom, didn’t _you_ think Aunt Sally ’d open out her arms and say, ‘Sid Sawyer—’” “My land!” she says, breaking in and jumping for him, “you impudent young rascal, to fool a body so—” and was going to hug him, but he fended her off, and says: “No, not till you’ve asked me first.” So she didn’t lose no time, but asked him; and hugged him and kissed him over and over again, and then turned him over to the old man, and he took what was left.
