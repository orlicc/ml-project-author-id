They gained steadily on it, and at last, breast to breast, they burst through the open door and fell grateful and exhausted in the sheltering shadows beyond. By and by their pulses slowed down, and Tom whispered: “Huckleberry, what do you reckon’ll come of this?” “If Doctor Robinson dies, I reckon hanging’ll come of it.” “Do you though?” “Why, I _know_ it, Tom.” Tom thought a while, then he said: “Who’ll tell? We?” “What are you talking about? S’pose something happened and Injun Joe _didn’t_ hang? Why, he’d kill us some time or other, just as dead sure as we’re a laying here.” “That’s just what I was thinking to myself, Huck.” “If anybody tells, let Muff Potter do it, if he’s fool enough.
