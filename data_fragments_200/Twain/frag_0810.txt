Do you know Robin Hood, Huck?” “No. Who’s Robin Hood?” “Why, he was one of the greatest men that was ever in England—and the best. He was a robber.” “Cracky, I wisht I was. Who did he rob?” “Only sheriffs and bishops and rich people and kings, and such like. But he never bothered the poor. He loved ’em. He always divided up with ’em perfectly square.” “Well, he must ’a’ been a brick.” “I bet you he was, Huck. Oh, he was the noblest man that ever was. They ain’t any such men now, I can tell you.
