As it was something to feel certain that assistance was sent for, and that no time had been lost, Oliver hurried up the inn-yard, with a somewhat lighter heart. He was turning out of the gateway when he accidently stumbled against a tall man wrapped in a cloak, who was at that moment coming out of the inn door. “Hah!” cried the man, fixing his eyes on Oliver, and suddenly recoiling. “What the devil’s this?” “I beg your pardon, sir,” said Oliver; “I was in a great hurry to get home, and didn’t see you were coming.” “Death!” muttered the man to himself, glaring at the boy with his large dark eyes. “Who would have thought it! Grind him to ashes!
