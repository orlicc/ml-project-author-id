He was startled when they came upon him while he was engaged in this latter pastime, and his colour changed. ‘Halloa!’ he stammered; ‘I didn’t know you were here.’ ‘Whose name, Tom,’ said Mr. Harthouse, putting his hand upon his shoulder and turning him, so that they all three walked towards the house together, ‘have you been carving on the trees?’ ‘Whose name?’ returned Tom. ‘Oh! You mean what girl’s name?’ ‘You have a suspicious appearance of inscribing some fair creature’s on the bark, Tom.’ [Picture: Mr. Harthouse and Tom Gradgrind in the garden] ‘Not much of that, Mr. Harthouse, unless some fair creature with a slashing fortune at her own disposal would take a fancy to me. Or she might be as ugly as she was rich, without any fear of losing me.
