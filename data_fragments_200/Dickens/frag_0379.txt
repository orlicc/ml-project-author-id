Even when their eyes had met, and her sister had approached the bed, Louisa lay for minutes looking at her in silence, and suffering her timidly to hold her passive hand, before she asked: ‘When was I brought to this room?’ ‘Last night, Louisa.’ ‘Who brought me here?’ ‘Sissy, I believe.’ ‘Why do you believe so?’ ‘Because I found her here this morning. She didn’t come to my bedside to wake me, as she always does; and I went to look for her. She was not in her own room either; and I went looking for her all over the house, until I found her here taking care of you and cooling your head. Will you see father? Sissy said I was to tell him when you woke.’ ‘What a beaming face you have, Jane!’ said Louisa, as her young sister—timidly still—bent down to kiss her. ‘Have I? I am very glad you think so.
