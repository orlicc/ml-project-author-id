In my pain an’ trouble, lookin up yonder,—wi’ it shinin on me—I ha’ seen more clear, and ha’ made it my dyin prayer that aw th’ world may on’y coom toogether more, an’ get a better unnerstan’in o’ one another, than when I were in ’t my own weak seln.’ Louisa hearing what he said, bent over him on the opposite side to Rachael, so that he could see her. ‘You ha’ heard?’ he said, after a few moments’ silence. ‘I ha’ not forgot you, ledy.’ ‘Yes, Stephen, I have heard you. And your prayer is mine.’ ‘You ha’ a father. Will yo tak’ a message to him?’ ‘He is here,’ said Louisa, with dread. ‘Shall I bring him to you?’ ‘If yo please.’ Louisa returned with her father.
