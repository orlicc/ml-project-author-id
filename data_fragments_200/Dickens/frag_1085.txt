To him, Nancy repeated her request. “What name am I to say?” asked the waiter. “It’s of no use saying any,” replied Nancy. “Nor business?” said the man. “No, nor that neither,” rejoined the girl. “I must see the lady.” “Come!” said the man, pushing her towards the door. “None of this. Take yourself off.” “I shall be carried out if I go!” said the girl violently; “and I can make that a job that two of you won’t like to do. Isn’t there anybody here,” she said, looking round, “that will see a simple message carried for a poor wretch like me?” This appeal produced an effect on a good-tempered-faced man-cook, who with some of the other servants was looking on, and who stepped forward to interfere. “Take it up for her, Joe; can’t you?” said this person. “What’s the good?” replied the man. “You don’t suppose the young lady will see such as her; do you?” This allusion to Nancy’s doubtful character, raised a vast
