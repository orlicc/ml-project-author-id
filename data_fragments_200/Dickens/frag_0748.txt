And all the infant paupers might have chorussed the rejoinder with great propriety, if they had heard it. “A porochial life, ma’am,” continued Mr. Bumble, striking the table with his cane, “is a life of worrit, and vexation, and hardihood; but all public characters, as I may say, must suffer prosecution.” Mrs. Mann, not very well knowing what the beadle meant, raised her hands with a look of sympathy, and sighed. “Ah! You may well sigh, Mrs. Mann!” said the beadle. Finding she had done right, Mrs. Mann sighed again: evidently to the satisfaction of the public character: who, repressing a complacent smile by looking sternly at his cocked hat, said, “Mrs. Mann, I am going to London.” “Lauk, Mr. Bumble!” cried Mrs. Mann, starting back. “To London, ma’am,” resumed the inflexible beadle, “by coach.
