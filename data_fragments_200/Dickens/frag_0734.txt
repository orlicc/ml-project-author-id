You shall have the books.” “If that ain’t mine!” said Bill Sikes, putting on his hat with a determined air; “mine and Nancy’s that is; I’ll take the boy back again.” The Jew started. Oliver started too, though from a very different cause; for he hoped that the dispute might really end in his being taken back. “Come! Hand over, will you?” said Sikes. “This is hardly fair, Bill; hardly fair, is it, Nancy?” inquired the Jew. “Fair, or not fair,” retorted Sikes, “hand over, I tell you! Do you think Nancy and me has got nothing else to do with our precious time but to spend it in scouting arter, and kidnapping, every young boy as gets grabbed through you?
