Sikes shrugged his shoulders impatiently, as if he thought the precaution unnecessary; but complied, nevertheless, by requesting Miss Nancy to fetch him a jug of beer. “You don’t want any beer,” said Nancy, folding her arms, and retaining her seat very composedly. “I tell you I do!” replied Sikes. “Nonsense,” rejoined the girl coolly, “Go on, Fagin. I know what he’s going to say, Bill; he needn’t mind me.” The Jew still hesitated. Sikes looked from one to the other in some surprise. “Why, you don’t mind the old girl, do you, Fagin?” he asked at length. “You’ve known her long enough to trust her, or the Devil’s in it. She ain’t one to blab.
