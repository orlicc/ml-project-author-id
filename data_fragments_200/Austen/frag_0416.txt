Those who are standing by are usually thinking of something very different.” This Emma felt was aimed at her; and it made her quite angry. It was not in compliment to Jane Fairfax however that he was so indifferent, or so indignant; he was not guided by _her_ feelings in reprobating the ball, for _she_ enjoyed the thought of it to an extraordinary degree. It made her animated—open hearted—she voluntarily said;— “Oh! Miss Woodhouse, I hope nothing may happen to prevent the ball. What a disappointment it would be!
